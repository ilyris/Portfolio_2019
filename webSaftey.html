Web Saftey.

Look at all the information I just grabbed from your browser

  1. Use HTTPS when submiting financial information

    When you're visiting a site you will notice in the top left corner of the URL there is a little HTTP or HTTPS before the domain name for the website you are on. This just leans HyperText Transfer Protolcol, at a pretty high level, this is how we send files to computers and servers on the web. The S in HTTP means secure. Meaning your information will be encrypted when it is used / sent to other applications.

  2. Limit your personal information on the web

    This can be thought about how you share information with a stranger, they don't need to know when your birthday is, your relationship status, what your hobbies are, and neighter does all the millions of people on the web. Keep this information limited on the web to remove yourself from the ability to have your personal information from being compromised.

  3. Stay away from pop-ups

    It's best to browse the web with ad-blockers. This removes the possibility from getting hit with a bunch of ads and possible pop-ups. Stay away from pop-ups and ads when browsing website so you don't pick up any malicious software as you surf the web. Also it is best to search for the thing you're looking for directly and finding a secure safe website. Google works hard to be sure users are safe from malicious software here is a fantast site I found with their statistics on how many harmful sites they have found https://transparencyreport.google.com/safe-browsing/overview

  4. Stay aware of what you download

    You want to make sure you are downloading software from a secure credible site. Cybercriminals love creating mock applications (copies) to install malicious software on your computer. Don't download programs from sketchy sites.

  5. Use strong passwords and don't save your passwords

    You as a user should be using multiple passwords for multiple sites, try not to reuse passwords as this is easier for hackers to crack your password and steal personal information from multiple websites. Hackers have the ability to hijack your browser session and steal information if you are still logged in to a site.

  6. Make sure your browser has been updated to the most recent information

    Same with any software, patches are realeased not only to fix bugs in their program, but these patches can also include security updates which stop the program / and your personal information from being compromised.

  7. Don't rely on your browser to protect you

    The best a web browser can do is to tell you that a website seems unsafe and you shouldn't browse it, the browser can't stop you from browseing the site.
